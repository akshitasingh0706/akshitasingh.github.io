Projects


I have worked on various projects in the Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, Deep Learning, and Developmental Economics space. I discuss some of them here.

Drift Detection in Text Data

During the course of my summer internship at Dana Farber Cancer Institute, I was fortunate to have worked implementing and deploying some data and model drift detection methods. Although the details and the data for that project are PII, I implemented a similar approach for an NLP project during my grad school, which I chose to name a href="https://github.com/akshitasingh0706/NaturallyDrifted"> Naturally Drifted.

Predicting Mental Health using Wearable Devices

During my grad school, I worked as a Graduate Student Researcher with Professor Amir Rahmani's HealthSci Tech Group. During my time, I helped develop some Machine Learning models to predict different mental health outcomes, using data collected from wearable devices (smart watches). The overarching gaols of this project and other details about the models and can be found href="https://github.com/mitmedialab/PersonalizedMultitaskLearning"> here

Detecing Traumatic Brain Injury using Electroencephalograph

We attempted to address the challenge of medical data accessibility by implementing Transfer Learning. In particular, we trained our model - which was a basic classification task for identifying traumatic brain injuries from EEG signals - on mice data, and then applied Transfer Learning on human data, which was much smaller in size.

Classifying Reddit Posts in Mental Health Buckets

As part of Accenture's AI for Social Good initiative, I worked on topic modeling project wherein we classified different Reddit posts into a set of mental health buckets.
